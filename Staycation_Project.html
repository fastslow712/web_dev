Capital Reef Natinal Park

Capital Reef is a national park. It was made into a park because of its natural beauty as it was formed from red rock that was eroded into a series of canyons. In addition it has a historical significances as it was home to an old community called Fruitas and still has its old orchards. It is filled with places to hike which is one of its best appeals.

Reasons to Go

  • Visitors can find a wide variety of formations in stone carved from wind and water errosion and landscape views
  • A number of Historical sites including Native American rock art and early settlement buildings and orchards
  • Several hiking trails for all levels of skill and ability from backcoundtry to riverside stroll are located in the park
  • Fresh baked items and other souvenirs sold from the kitchen of a historic home located in a productive fruit orchard

Additional Inforamation

Capitol Reef is about four hours from Zion and Bryce National Parks. It is three hours from Arches and Canyonlands National Parks. It touches Grand Staircase-Escalante Natinal Monument. Torrey and Hanksville are good towns to stay in while visiting the park.
